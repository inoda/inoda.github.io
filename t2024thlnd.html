Overview

Spent 4 weeks in San Kamphaeng - about 30 mins outside of Chiang Mai - training at Santai Muay Thai gym. Aside from the training and food (of course), a highlight for me was reading Radical Acceptance (by Tara Brach). It really impacted me. Before leaving, I joked that I was going into "monk mode". Turned out to be pretty accurate!

First couple weeks

For the first two weeks, the only time I wasn't dripping sweat was when I was sitting directly in front of a fan. That's Thailand's winter for you.

The gym had people from all over the world. Interesting demographic notes: at one point there were 4 people from Chicago, and 2 chefs from France.

Between training, working, and eating, I had almost zero free time. Every Sunday was a rest day and I'd get a haircut + massage. Otherwise my day would look like this:

  • · Wake up at 5:30, go to gym while it's still dark out
  • · Morning session, ~2 hours (3mi run, warmup, hour of technique drills, then abs)
  • · Get food and read
  • · Work
  • · Afternoon session, ~2.5 hours (warmup, bag work, 3 rounds on pads, clinching, then abs)
  • · Walk to main street for dinner (usually Khao Man Gai and Pad See Ewe)
  • · Work

San Kamphaeng and Chiang Mai

San Kamphaeng is a little town. There's basically one main street. It felt really peaceful, like a village in a Miyazaki movie. As you walk around you hear animals, bugs buzzing, people chattering. Any foreigner you see on the street is there for Muay Thai training. Every Saturday evening, the town would come alive with a big street market that went on for blocks.

I only went to Chiang Mai twice. Once to watch guys from the gym fight. The other time to go to a silk factory and street market. I enjoyed the fights less than I thought, and the silk factory more than I thought.

Last couple weeks

For the second half, I cut out the morning sessions. I was staying up late to work normal US hours and my body was feeling the training...mainly my hip flexors (from being inflexible and kicking a lot) and my knee (from a few knee-on-knees in clinching). By the end, I'm still a total noob but learned a ton. Except for the last few days (I caught a cold), my days looked like this.

  • · Wake up at noon, food and read, maybe get massage
  • · Work
  • · Afternoon session
  • · Food
  • · Work til 4am
